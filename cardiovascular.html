Overview

The cardiovascular system consists of the heart, blood vessels, and blood, working together to transport essential substances throughout the body.

  • Functions: Delivers oxygen and nutrients, removes waste, regulates temperature, and maintains pH balance.
  • Importance: Essential for sustaining life and supporting all other body systems.

Heart Anatomy

The heart is a muscular organ that pumps blood through the circulatory system.

  • Atria: Upper chambers that receive blood.
  • Ventricles: Lower chambers that pump blood out.
  • Valves: Ensure one-way blood flow (e.g., mitral, tricuspid, aortic, pulmonary).
  • Coronary Arteries: Supply oxygen-rich blood to the heart muscle.
  • Pericardium: Protective sac surrounding the heart.
  • Septum: Wall dividing the left and right sides of the heart.

Blood Circulation

Blood circulates through two primary pathways:

  • Systemic Circulation: Oxygenated blood is delivered to tissues.
  • Pulmonary Circulation: Blood is oxygenated in the lungs.

Cardiac Cycle: The sequence of events in one heartbeat, including contraction (systole) and relaxation (diastole).

Types of Blood Vessels

  • Arteries: Carry oxygen-rich blood away from the heart.
  • Veins: Return deoxygenated blood to the heart.
  • Capillaries: Facilitate exchange of gases and nutrients.
  • Venules & Arterioles: Small vessels connecting capillaries to veins and arteries.

Blood Components

  • Red Blood Cells: Transport oxygen via hemoglobin.
  • White Blood Cells: Defend against infections.
  • Platelets: Aid in blood clotting.
  • Plasma: Fluid carrying nutrients, hormones, and waste.

Fun Fact: An adult has about 5 liters of blood circulating in their body.

Common Cardiovascular Disorders

  • Hypertension: High blood pressure affecting arteries.
  • Atherosclerosis: Plaque buildup narrowing blood vessels.
  • Heart Attack: Blockage of coronary arteries leading to tissue damage.
  • Stroke: Disruption of blood flow to the brain.
  • Arrhythmia: Irregular heartbeat.
  • Heart Failure: Heart's inability to pump blood efficiently.

How to Keep Your Cardiovascular System Healthy

  • Exercise regularly (at least 150 minutes per week).
  • Eat a balanced diet low in saturated fats and salt.
  • Avoid smoking and limit alcohol consumption.
  • Manage stress and get enough sleep.
  • Monitor blood pressure and cholesterol levels.
